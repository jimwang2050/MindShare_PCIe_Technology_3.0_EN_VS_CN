## **Limitations of 66 MHz PCI bus** 

While the throughput of the bus was doubled at this speed relative to the 33 MHz bus, the diagram illustrates one of its major shortcomings: using the same reflected‐wave switching model with only half the timing budget meant that the loading on the bus had to be greatly reduced. The result was that only one add‐in card could be supported on each bus. Adding more device meant add‐ ing more PCI bridges and buses would increases both cost and board real estate requirements. The 64‐bit PCI bus increases pin count, increasing system cost and lowered system reliability. In combination, it’s easy to see why these factors limited the popularity of 64‐bit or 66 MHz version of PCI bus.
